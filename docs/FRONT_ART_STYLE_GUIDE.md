# Front Art Style Guide

## Direction

Front art is cinematic environment concept art for a modern eastern historical-fantasy strategy game. It combines credible terrain and architecture with restrained archive-like future detail. The common palette uses ink black, blue gray, weathered bronze, limited copper-gold light, and small cinnabar accents.

The images depict places, not character portraits. Human figures may appear only as distant scale references without recognizable identity. The scene remains usable behind interactive cards and rules.

## Composition

- Landscape ratio is 3:2 with a consistent elevated tactical camera.
- Atmospheric perspective separates foreground, playable middle space, and distant landmark.
- The thematic landmark belongs above or beside the central card area.
- The center and lower third remain calm enough for card and text contrast after a dark overlay.
- Detail density, horizon height, realism, and fantasy intensity remain consistent across the set.
- Lighting may vary by category, but highlights remain restrained and avoid broad glare.

## Gameplay motifs

| Mechanic | Preferred motifs |
| --- | --- |
| Movement | Roads, bridges, currents, converging routes, rotating formations |
| Cost and supply | Warehouses, gates, foundries, ledgers, docks, measured resources |
| Death and return | Empty memorial ground, abandoned standards, solemn ruins, returning light |
| Hidden information | Fog, screened camps, distant watch posts, enclosed valleys |
| Capacity and restriction | Narrow passes, expanding camps, single gates, heavy fortifications |
| Era and region | Material culture, architecture, observatories, forums, beacons |
| Endgame and reversal | Closing terrain, balancing tide, inverted spatial rhythm, migrating light |

Symbols must remain environmental and non-linguistic. A mechanic cannot be represented with labels, digits, interface arrows, meters, text panels, or card frames.

## Prohibited content

No image may contain readable text, numbers, logos, signatures, watermarks, interface elements, card borders, commercial characters, close-up portraits, graphic injury, blood, bodies, or horror imagery. Memorial and death mechanics are conveyed through empty space, damaged objects, weather, and light.

## Review standard

Every front is reviewed for subject match, unique composition, family consistency, safe lower-third readability, absence of prohibited elements, correct crop at all three output sizes, and sufficient contrast under the game overlay. A technically invalid image is replaced once per approved retry; unrelated accepted images are not regenerated.
