# Game Design

Aeonfront is a two-player tactical card game designed for three-to-six-minute matches. Its identity is the collision of historical, legendary, fictional and modern figures inside a modern strategic archive presentation.

## Match arc

Each player brings twelve unique cards. Three different fronts are selected with a seed and revealed during turns one, two and three. Both players privately plan any number of affordable deployments, revise them, then lock independently. The initiative holder reveals first and each player's cards preserve submitted order. After turn six, controlling two fronts wins; otherwise total power breaks a one-front-each tie.

Military orders equal the current turn and do not carry over. The resulting curve creates an opening reconnaissance phase, a midgame of front commitment and a high-impact finale.

## Original pressure systems

Initiative is earned by controlling more fronts, then total power, with ties retaining the prior holder. Raising a banner announces confidence and schedules the stake increase for the next turn, leaving a response window in which the opponent can withdraw at the current stake. The sixth turn doubles the active stake automatically.

The product does not sell competitive power, use real currency or resolve outcomes with generated text. Optional post-game chronicle generation is presentation only, remains disabled by default and cannot alter the event log or winner.
